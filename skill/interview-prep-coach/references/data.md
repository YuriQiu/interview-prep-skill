# Data Interview Reference

## Scorecard Areas

For data roles, evaluate:

- SQL and data modeling.
- Metrics definition.
- Experiment design and causal reasoning.
- Statistical judgment.
- Business problem framing.
- Communication with non-technical stakeholders.
- Data quality and analytical rigor.

## Analytics Case Flow

Use this structure:

1. Clarify the business question.
2. Define the target metric and guardrail metrics.
3. Segment the problem.
4. Identify data sources and quality risks.
5. Propose analysis or experiment.
6. Explain expected outputs.
7. Recommend an action and follow-up.

## SQL Practice Areas

Generate questions around:

- Joins and aggregations.
- Window functions.
- Funnels and retention.
- Cohort analysis.
- Deduplication.
- Time-series metrics.
- Experiment readouts.

## Experiment Questions

For A/B testing prompts, cover:

- Hypothesis.
- Unit of randomization.
- Primary metric.
- Guardrails.
- Power or sample-size intuition.
- Novelty, seasonality, and interference risks.
- Decision criteria.

## Communication Standard

Strong data answers translate analysis into a decision. Avoid only describing
queries or methods. End with what the business should do next and what evidence
would change the recommendation.
