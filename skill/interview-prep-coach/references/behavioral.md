# Behavioral Interview Reference

## Answer Structures

Use STAR when the user needs a complete behavioral answer:

- Situation: enough context to understand stakes.
- Task: the user's responsibility, not the team's responsibility.
- Action: specific decisions, tradeoffs, communication, and execution.
- Result: measurable outcome, lesson, or durable change.

Use CAR for shorter answers:

- Context.
- Action.
- Result.

## Story Bank

Build 5-7 reusable stories:

- Ownership under ambiguity.
- Technical or operational challenge.
- Conflict or disagreement.
- Failure, mistake, or missed goal.
- Influence without authority.
- Cross-functional collaboration.
- Learning, growth, or resilience.

For each story, capture:

- Title.
- Role and scope.
- Stakes.
- Hardest decision.
- Actions.
- Result.
- What the user learned.
- Variants for leadership, teamwork, conflict, and failure prompts.

## Feedback Rubric

Strong answers are:

- Specific enough that the interviewer can picture the work.
- Honest about tradeoffs and constraints.
- Focused on the user's actions.
- Connected to business, user, technical, or team impact.
- Concise: usually 90-150 seconds spoken.

Risk signs:

- "We" without explaining the user's contribution.
- No measurable or observable result.
- Blaming others.
- Overexplaining background.
- Claiming impact that does not match the user's actual authority.

## Common Questions

- Tell me about yourself.
- Why this company?
- Why this role?
- Tell me about a difficult project.
- Tell me about a time you failed.
- Tell me about a conflict with a teammate or stakeholder.
- Tell me about a time you influenced without authority.
- What feedback have you received?
- What are you looking for in your next role?
